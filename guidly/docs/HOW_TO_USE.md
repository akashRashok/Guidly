# How to Use Guidly

## For Teachers

### Getting Started

1. **Sign In**
   - Go to the login page
   - Enter your email address
   - In development mode, you'll be logged in immediately
   - In production, check your email for a magic link

2. **Access Your Dashboard**
   - After signing in, you'll see your dashboard
   - This shows all your assignments
   - Click "New Assignment" to create your first homework

### Creating an Assignment

1. **Fill in Assignment Details**
   - **Title**: Give your assignment a clear name (e.g., "Week 3 Fractions Practice")
   - **Topic**: Select from available topics:
     - Fractions
     - Algebra
     - Percentages
     - Forces (Physics)
     - Energy (Physics)
     - Electricity (Physics)
     - Grammar (English)
     - General

2. **Add Questions**
   
   **Option A: Manual Entry**
   - Click "Add First Question" or "Add Another Question"
   - Enter the question text
   - Enter the correct answer
   - Repeat for all questions

   **Option B: Use Templates**
   - Select a topic first
   - Click "Use Templates" button
   - Review the available template questions
   - Click "Add All Templates" to add them all
   - You can still add more questions manually

3. **Create the Assignment**
   - Click "Create Assignment"
   - You'll be taken to the assignment results page
   - **Important**: Copy the homework link and class code

4. **Share with Students**
   - Share the homework link (e.g., `http://localhost:3000/homework/a3k9m2p`)
   - Share the 4-character class code (e.g., `7B2K`)
   - Students need both to start the assignment

### Viewing Results

1. **Access Assignment Results**
   - From your dashboard, click on any assignment
   - You'll see the results summary page

2. **Understanding the Summary**
   - **Stats Overview**: Total students, completion count, number of questions, accuracy percentage
   - **Teaching Recommendation**: AI-generated suggestion for what to revisit (if misconceptions found)
   - **Common Misconceptions**: List of top misconceptions with counts
   - **Student List**: All students who started, with completion status and scores

3. **Using the Information**
   - Focus on the most common misconceptions
   - Use the teaching recommendation to plan your next lesson
   - Identify students who may need extra help

### Closing an Assignment

1. **When to Close**
   - After the submission deadline
   - When you've collected enough data
   - When you want to prevent new submissions

2. **How to Close**
   - Go to the assignment results page
   - Click "Close Assignment" button
   - Confirm the action
   - Once closed, students can no longer start new sessions

## For Students

### Starting Homework

1. **Get the Link and Code**
   - Your teacher will share:
     - A homework link (URL)
     - A 4-character class code

2. **Open the Link**
   - Click the homework link or paste it in your browser
   - You'll see the assignment entry page

3. **Enter Your Details**
   - **Your Name**: Enter your first name and last initial (e.g., "Sarah T")
   - **Class Code**: Enter the 4-character code from your teacher (e.g., "7B2K")
   - Click "Start Homework"

### Answering Questions

1. **Read the Question**
   - Each question appears one at a time
   - You can see your progress at the top

2. **Submit Your Answer**
   - Type your answer in the text box
   - Click "Submit Answer"
   - The system will check your answer

3. **If Your Answer is Correct**
   - You'll move to the next question automatically
   - Your progress updates

4. **If Your Answer is Incorrect**
   - You'll see a feedback screen explaining why your answer is wrong
   - The explanation identifies the misconception
   - Read the explanation carefully
   - Click "I Understand â€” Continue" when ready

5. **Follow-Up Question**
   - After reading the explanation, you'll see a follow-up question
   - This tests if you understood the feedback
   - Answer the follow-up question
   - Then you'll move to the next question

### Completing Homework

1. **Finish All Questions**
   - Answer all questions in order
   - You can't skip questions
   - You can't go back to previous questions

2. **Completion Summary**
   - After the last question, you'll see a completion screen
   - It shows how many questions you got correct on your first try
   - You can close the page

3. **What Happens Next**
   - Your teacher can see your responses
   - Your teacher will use this information to plan lessons
   - You don't need to do anything else

## Tips for Teachers

### Creating Effective Assignments

1. **Keep It Focused**
   - One topic per assignment
   - 3-5 questions is usually enough
   - Focus on concepts, not just procedures

2. **Use Clear Questions**
   - Write questions that test understanding, not just recall
   - Make sure correct answers are unambiguous
   - Consider common wrong answers when writing questions

3. **Choose Appropriate Topics**
   - Select topics that align with your current teaching
   - Use the topic to help the system identify misconceptions
   - General topic works if none of the specific topics fit

### Interpreting Results

1. **Look for Patterns**
   - Multiple students with the same misconception = teaching opportunity
   - One student struggling = individual support needed
   - High accuracy = students understood the concept

2. **Use the Recommendations**
   - The AI-generated summary suggests what to revisit
   - Focus on the most common misconceptions first
   - Don't try to address everything at once

3. **Follow Up in Class**
   - Use the misconception data to plan your next lesson
   - Address the most common issues
   - Provide additional examples or explanations

## Tips for Students

### Getting the Most from Feedback

1. **Read Carefully**
   - Don't skip the explanation
   - Try to understand why your answer was wrong
   - Think about the misconception mentioned

2. **Use the Follow-Up**
   - The follow-up question tests your understanding
   - Take your time to think about it
   - It's okay if you're still learning

3. **Don't Worry About Mistakes**
   - Making mistakes is part of learning
   - The feedback helps you understand
   - Your teacher uses this information to help everyone

## Troubleshooting

### For Teachers

**Problem**: Can't see my assignments
- **Solution**: Make sure you're signed in with the correct email

**Problem**: Students can't access the homework
- **Solution**: 
  - Check that the link is correct
  - Verify the class code matches
  - Make sure the assignment isn't closed

**Problem**: No misconceptions showing
- **Solution**: 
  - Students may have answered correctly
  - Wait for more students to complete
  - Check that questions are appropriate for the topic

### For Students

**Problem**: Can't start the homework
- **Solution**: 
  - Check the link is correct
  - Verify the class code (it's case-sensitive)
  - Make sure the assignment isn't closed
  - Contact your teacher if issues persist

**Problem**: Answer not accepted as correct
- **Solution**: 
  - Check spelling and formatting
  - Try different formats (e.g., "3/4" vs "0.75")
  - The system is flexible but exact matches work best

**Problem**: Feedback not clear
- **Solution**: 
  - Read the explanation carefully
  - Ask your teacher for clarification
  - The feedback is designed to help you understand

## Best Practices

### For Teachers

1. **Timing**: Create assignments after teaching a concept, not before
2. **Frequency**: Use sparingly - quality over quantity
3. **Follow-up**: Always review results and address misconceptions in class
4. **Closure**: Close assignments after the deadline to prevent late submissions

### For Students

1. **Honesty**: Use your real name (or the format your teacher requested)
2. **Focus**: Take your time and think through each question
3. **Learning**: Use feedback to understand, not just to complete
4. **Communication**: Ask your teacher if you're confused

## Privacy & Data

- **Student Names**: Only visible to your teacher, never in URLs
- **Data Scope**: Your responses exist only for this assignment
- **No Tracking**: The system doesn't track you across assignments
- **Teacher Access**: Only your teacher can see your responses

---

Remember: Guidly is designed to help you learn, not to judge. Focus on understanding, and use the feedback to improve.
